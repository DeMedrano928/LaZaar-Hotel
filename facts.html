Interesting Facts and Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q: What is a Capybara?
  • A: Capybaras are the largest rodent in the world, they're native to South and known to live semi-aquatic lifestyles and living near bodies of water.

  • Q: What do Capybaras eat?
  • A: Capybara's diets primarily consist of grass, water plants, cultivated vegetables, hay, and more.

  • Q: How large do they grow up to be as adults?
  • A: They can grow up to be 130 cm long on and weigh the same as an average human adult, once fully grown into adulthood.

  • Q: How do Capybaras swim so fast?
  • A: They're known to be excellent swimmers due to their webbed feet and can hold their breath underwater for up to 5 minutes on average.

  • Q: What animals do they interact with?
  • A: They show no hostility towards any animals and enjoy the company of many other animals such as birds, meerkats, cats, crocodiles, ducks, turtles, and much more.

  • Q: What are some nicknames for capybaras?
  • A: Birds and other animals are known for hitching a ride on capybaras, giving capybaras the name, "Moving Chairs" or "Nature's Ottoman".

  • Q: How fast are capybaras on land?
  • A: Capybaras are not only fast swimmers but fast runners as well, they're capable of reaching up to the speed of a horse, 35 kph or 21.75 mph.

  • Q: How do their teeth adapt to the constant grinding?
  • A: Their teeth always continuously grow to advocate for the constant wearing of their teeth from eating tough aquatic plants.

  • Q: Can you keep a Capybara as a pet?
  • A: In some countries and states of the U.S. it is illegal to own a Capybara, however in countries and states that are legal have many restrictions and limitations to when you can keep a capybara.
